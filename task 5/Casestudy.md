# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attacks

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is basically when hackers try to trick u into giving them ur personal info by pretending to be someone trustworthy. Like they might send u a fake email that looks like its from ur bank or ur college or something, and u think its real so u click the link and put in ur password. Yeah its pretty dumb when u think about it but it works on so many ppl honestly.

## How It Usually Happens (Step by Step)

Ok so heres how these scams typically go down. First the hacker sends out like thousands of emails that look super legit. They copy the logo and everything from the real company so it seems official right? Then in the email theyre like "ur account has been compromised!!" or "verify ur info asap" which makes ppl panic. When u click the link it takes u to a fake website thats basically identical to the real one. Most ppl dont even notice lol. Then u put in ur username and password thinking ur logging in, but actually the hacker is just stealing it. After that they have access to ur actual account and can do whatever they want.

## Who Gets Targeted

College students r actually like super easy targets for this stuff. Were always in a rush and dont really check emails carefully. Plus older ppl also fall for it cuz theyre not as familiar with how the internet works. Banks and paypal and stuff get copied the most because those have access to ur money. Students r also targeted with fake emails pretending to be from their university portal or library system.

## What Happens After (The Consequences)

The consequences r honestly really bad. If someone gets ur bank info they can literally steal ur money which is the worst. Or they can use ur identity to open credit cards and get loans in ur name. That takes forever to fix btw like ur credit score gets messed up for years. Some ppl end up losing thousands of dollars. Plus ur personal files and photos can be stolen and shared. And theres like mental stress from knowing someone violated ur privacy. A lot of ppl feel really stupid after it happens even tho its not their fault cuz these phishing emails r getting better and better at looking real.

## How To Not Fall For It

The best way to not get scammed is to like actually check the email address and hover over links before u click them. If something seems sketchy just call the company directly or go to their website urself instead of clicking the email link. Banks will literally never ask u for ur password in an email so if u see that its def fake. Just be paranoid about it basically lol.
