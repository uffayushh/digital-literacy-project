# Stay Safe Online - Prevention Checklist for Indian College Students

ok yaar so heres all the stuff u actually need to do to not get hacked or scammed in india. ive added tips that actually matter for us college students lol

## 8+ Actionable Prevention Tips

### 1. **Never Share Ur OTP (One Time Password)**
- [ ] no matter who asks u for ur OTP dont give it. like literally never
- [ ] ur bank will never ask for OTP over the phone or email its a trick
- [ ] if someone is asking for OTP on whatsapp/telegram theyre 100% scamming u
- [ ] even if they say theyre from the bank or police just hang up lol

### 2. **Protect Ur UPI Apps Like Theyre Gold**
- [ ] dont let anyone use ur phone when paytm/googlepay/phonepe is open
- [ ] set a strong PIN on ur upi app not like "1234" or ur birthdate
- [ ] check ur transaction history regularly cuz sometimes ppl steal money
- [ ] if u see weird transactions report it immediately to ur bank

### 3. **Be Super Careful With Public WiFi**
- [ ] literally do NOT do banking or upi transfers on public college wifi
- [ ] hackers can see ur passwords on public networks its not safe
- [ ] if u must use public wifi use a vpn (like express vpn or something)
- [ ] better just wait and use mobile data honestly

### 4. **Use Strong Passwords and Dont Reuse Them**
- [ ] make passwords that r like atleast 12 characters long with numbers and symbols
- [ ] dont use the same password for instagram and ur bank account thats dumb
- [ ] change ur important passwords like every 3 months ish
- [ ] write them down in a notebook at home or use a password manager

### 5. **Watch Out For Fake College Portals and Forms**
- [ ] theres lots of fake google forms going around pretending to be from ur college
- [ ] always check the link before clicking it look at the email address
- [ ] if ur college asks for passwords thats sus they literally never do that
- [ ] ask ur seniors or friends if theyre getting the same email first

### 6. **Verify Before Transferring Money on UPI**
- [ ] seriously check the name on upi before u send money like 5 times
- [ ] if someone asks for money over whatsapp for an "emergency" call them first
- [ ] theres lots of fake upi ids that look like ur friends names so be careful
- [ ] if something feels off it probably is ngl just ask them directly

### 7. **SIM Card Swap Fraud is Real So Protect Ur Phone Number**
- [ ] dont share ur phone number on random websites or apps
- [ ] scammers can convince ur telecom company to give them ur number
- [ ] then they can reset ur bank passwords and steal everything
- [ ] ask ur telecom provider to put a lock on ur account (airtel/jio/idea all have this)
- [ ] keep ur aadhar and phone number details private

### 8. **Don't Click Links From Unknown People**
- [ ] if u get a message on whatsapp/insta from someone u dont know dont click anything
- [ ] links can be malware and install stuff on ur phone
- [ ] even if ur friend sends u a sus link ask them if they actually sent it
- [ ] screenshot weird messages and show them to someone before clicking

### 9. **Check Ur Bank Account Statements Regularly**
- [ ] like atleast once a week check whats happening in ur account
- [ ] if u see money going out that u didnt send block ur card immediately
- [ ] call ur bank's helpline (its on the back of ur card) right away
- [ ] dont wait around cuz the faster u report the better

### 10. **Be Careful About What Info U Put On Social Media**
- [ ] dont post ur aadhar, pan card, or any id document online even as a joke
- [ ] dont post screenshots of ur bank account or upi balance
- [ ] scammers can use this info to impersonate u
- [ ] just keep that stuff private honestly its not worth the likes

## If Something Bad Happens Report It ASAP

### National Cyber Crime Portal
- **Website:** cybercrime.gov.in
- **What to do:** go on this site and file a complaint immediately if ur hacked or scammed
- **Its free:** u dont have to pay anything to report
- **They actually help:** the police actually look at these complaints and try to catch the scammers

### Cyber Crime Helpline
- **Call:** 1930 (works from any phone in india)
- **Available:** 24/7 so u can call anytime
- **What they do:** they give u advice on what to do and help u file complaints
- **Talk to someone:** if ur panicking and dont know what to do just call them honestly theyre really helpful

### Also Tell Ur Bank
- [ ] call ur bank immediately on the number on ur debit/credit card
- [ ] tell them what happened and they can block transactions
- [ ] ask them to reverse any charges that werent ur fault
- [ ] keep the complaint number they give u for ur records
